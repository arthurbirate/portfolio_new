Video Game Sales Analytics Dashboard

This project analyzes global video game sales data to uncover industry trends, regional performance differences, genre popularity, and publisher dominance. The goal was to transform raw historical sales data into interactive dashboards that support strategic market analysis and competitive benchmarking.

Note: All sales figures and revenue measures in this project are expressed in millions of units (M).

Project Objective

The primary objective was to build a comprehensive analytical solution that supports strategic and competitive decision-making across the global gaming market.

  • Measures overall market size and performance
  • Identifies top-performing games and genres
  • Compares sales performance across major regions
  • Evaluates publisher-level market concentration
  • Supports strategic and competitive decision-making

Tools & Technologies

  • Tableau Desktop — KPIs, parameters, filtering, treemaps, dual-axis charts
  • Tableau Prep — cleaning, transformation, aggregation, modeling
  • Calculated fields — custom metrics & logic
  • Pareto analysis — cumulative % & contribution logic
  • Top-N controls — interactive ranking exploration

Dashboards

Three dashboards designed for executive overview, regional performance analysis, and publisher benchmarking.

Global Sales Overview Dashboard

Executive Summary

The Global Sales Overview Dashboard serves as the executive summary of the entire video game market. Its primary purpose is to provide a macro-level understanding of industry performance using consolidated KPIs and trend analysis.

This dashboard displays total global sales (in millions), total number of games released, and average sales per game (in millions), giving immediate visibility into overall market scale and productivity.

A ranked bar chart highlights the top-selling games, allowing stakeholders to identify blockbuster titles that significantly influenced total market revenue. The genre distribution visualization shows which categories dominate sales contribution, helping identify long-term consumer preferences.

Additionally, a yearly sales trend line illustrates the industry’s growth trajectory, peak performance years, and subsequent decline phases. This time-series analysis supports strategic forecasting and historical performance evaluation.

Overall, this dashboard answers the question: How has the global video game market performed over time, and what are the key drivers of revenue?

Regional Sales Breakdown Dashboard

Regional Insights

The Regional Sales Dashboard focuses on comparing performance across North America, Europe, and Japan, with all sales values displayed in millions.

Its purpose is to analyze how revenue is geographically distributed and to uncover differences in consumer demand across markets. By visualizing top-selling games per region and allowing dynamic comparisons, this dashboard highlights regional preferences and performance gaps.

For example, certain franchises may dominate North America but show relatively lower performance in Japan. These insights are critical for localization strategies, regional marketing investments, and product launch planning.

This dashboard answers the question: Where is revenue concentrated geographically, and how do consumer preferences differ across regions?

Publishers Insight Dashboard

Competitive Benchmarking

The Publishers Insight Dashboard evaluates competitive positioning and market concentration within the industry.

A treemap visualization is used to represent publisher market share, where each rectangle’s size corresponds to total global sales (in millions). This visual structure allows immediate identification of dominant publishers, as larger rectangles represent higher revenue contribution.

Unlike a heatmap (which typically emphasizes intensity through color), the treemap emphasizes proportional market share through area size, making it ideal for demonstrating revenue concentration and competitive hierarchy.

In addition to the treemap, the dashboard includes ranked comparisons and cumulative percentage analysis (Pareto principle) to show how a relatively small number of publishers generate a large share of total market sales.

It answers the question: Who controls the largest share of the gaming market, and how concentrated is industry revenue?

Data Model Explanation

Star Schema Design

This data model follows a star schema design, optimized for analytical queries and dashboard performance in Tableau. At the center of the model is the Fact_Sales table, surrounded by multiple dimension tables that provide descriptive business context.

Fact Table: Fact_Sales

The Fact_Sales table is the core transactional table containing measurable business metrics:

  • Global Sales (in millions)
  • Regional Sales (NA, EU, JP – in millions)
  • Additional quantitative performance metrics

Each record represents a specific game’s sales performance and includes foreign keys linking to dimension tables, enabling multidimensional analysis.

Dimension: Dim_Calendar

Enables time-based analysis through year, month, and hierarchical time structures. Supports trend analysis, year-over-year comparisons, and time-series visualizations.

Dimension: Dim_Games

Contains descriptive information about each game, including title and related attributes. Enables filtering, ranking, and Top-N performance analysis.

Dimension: Dim_Genre

Categorizes games into segments such as Action, Shooter, Role-Playing, Sports, and Platform. Supports genre-level performance analysis and market share evaluation.

Dimension: Dim_Platforms

Provides information about gaming platforms including PlayStation, Xbox, Nintendo, and PC. Enables platform-level comparisons and ecosystem impact analysis.

Dimension: Dim_Publisher

Provides company-level context including publisher name and grouping. Supports competitive benchmarking, market concentration analysis, and revenue share evaluation.

Why This Star Schema Is Effective

  • Separates measurable metrics (facts) from descriptive attributes (dimensions)
  • Improves query performance and scalability
  • Enables flexible slicing and dicing across time, genre, region, platform, and publisher
  • Simplifies calculated measures and aggregations in Tableau
